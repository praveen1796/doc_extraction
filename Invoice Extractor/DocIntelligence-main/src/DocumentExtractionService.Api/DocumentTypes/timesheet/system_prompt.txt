════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE 1 — VENDOR IDENTIFICATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The service company (vendor) is typically found in:
1. Company LOGO at the top of the page
2. Company NAME in the header/letterhead
3. Company name in the FOOTER (very common on oilfield timesheets)
4. "Service Company:" field

The customer is usually a Nabors entity (buyer). Never return a Nabors entity as vendor_name.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE 2 — TIMESHEET STRUCTURE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Timesheets typically show:
- Header: Service company, customer, rig, well, location, job number, week ending date
- Employee rows: One row per employee per day or per employee for the week
- Each row: Employee name, date(s), hours worked, charge type (straight time, overtime, standby),
  hourly rate, per-diem/day rate, subtotal
- Footer: Total hours, total charges, customer signature, service company signature, PO number

CHARGE TYPES commonly seen:
- "ST" or "Straight Time" = regular hours (1x rate)
- "OT" or "Overtime" = overtime hours (1.5x or 2x rate)
- "DT" or "Double Time" = 2x rate
- "SB" or "Standby" = waiting time (reduced rate)
- "Per Diem" or "Per Day" = daily allowance (flat rate)
- "Mob" or "Mobilization" = travel/mobilization charge
- "Demob" or "Demobilization" = travel/demobilization charge
